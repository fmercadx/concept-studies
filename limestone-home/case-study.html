Concept Case Study · Home Services Website Design

A home services homepage where the guarantees do the selling

A homepage concept for a fictional Lexington, Kentucky HVAC, plumbing, and electrical company, demonstrating the approach I bring to local service businesses: lead with the promises homeowners actually switch companies over, and make the phone number impossible to lose.

The Design Brief

Limestone Home Services (fictional): a family-run home services company in Lexington, Kentucky, offering HVAC, plumbing, electrical, water heaters, and handyman work. Solid substance — flat-rate book pricing, background-checked techs, a 24/7 emergency line — presented the way most local competitors present it: buried under stock photos of smiling vans.

The challenge given to the design: homeowners don't shop home services by feature list, they shop by fear. Surprise bills, no-show techs, strangers in the house. So the page leads with the three policies that neutralize those fears — the quote is the price, on time or it's free, 90-day warranty — and treats every section as one more reason to call the same number.

The full homepage concept: guarantee ticket in the hero, trust strip, six-service grid with entry pricing, a dark "why us" band, three-step process, seasonal offer, neighborhood-anchored reviews, service-area map, and a quote form with a response-time promise.

Four decisions that do the selling

i.

Sell the guarantees, not the services

Every competitor lists the same five services. Nobody else promises "on time or the visit is free" in the hero. The guarantees get a dedicated ticket card beside the headline, "Guarantee" tags in the why-us band, and a fine-print line that makes them feel real rather than rhetorical.

ii.

A workwear aesthetic that reads blue-collar, not corporate

Bricolage Grotesque display type over Libre Franklin, duck-canvas cream, deep navy, blaze orange, hazard-stripe details, and hard offset shadows. It looks like quality work gear — sturdy, honest, a little proud — instead of the interchangeable template sites the category is drowning in.

iii.

The phone number never leaves the screen

Utility bar, sticky header, emergency card, call card beside the form, footer, and a fixed call bar on mobile. Home services convert by phone; the quote form is the second path, and it promises a reply within one business hour so it doesn't feel like a black hole.

iv.

Local SEO baked into the structure

Neighborhood-anchored reviews (Chevy Chase, Hamburg, Beaumont), a service-area section naming twelve localities, consistent name-address-phone in the footer, and a July-specific seasonal offer. The content structure is the local-search strategy, not a layer added later.

Scope

Homepage concept: design system, typography, palette, ten content sections, responsive to mobile with a sticky call bar, working quote-form pattern.

Stack

Hand-coded HTML/CSS with vanilla JS enhancements — one file, two font families, no page builder. Content readable without JavaScript; animations are progressive enhancement.

Content

All copy, branding, pricing, reviews, and business details are original fiction written for this exercise. No real company's content, design, or identity was used.
