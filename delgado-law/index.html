Why Timing Matters

Three things that get decided early — with or without you

By the time many injured workers call a lawyer, the insurer has already shaped the record. An early consultation puts these three levers back in your hands.

01

Your wages get verified

Your benefits are calculated from your average weekly wage. Get that number wrong at the start — overtime missed, second job ignored — and every check that follows is wrong too.

02

Your injury gets documented

What the first medical records say about how you were hurt follows your claim forever. We make sure the record reflects what actually happened — completely and accurately.

03

Your doctor gets chosen

In Virginia, the panel physician decision affects your treatment and your claim. Most workers don't know they have choices here. You should know yours before you commit.

These three conversations happen in your first free consultation — before the insurer locks in its version of events.

Common Questions

Answers you should have before you decide anything

Every case is different — these answers are general information, not legal advice. Your free consultation covers your specific situation.

What does it cost to hire a workers' comp lawyer?

The consultation is free, and workers' compensation fees in Virginia are contingent and subject to approval by the Workers' Compensation Commission — meaning fees come out of what we recover, not out of your pocket up front.

I already reported my injury. Is it too late to call?

No — but sooner is better. Even after reporting, decisions about wage calculation, medical treatment, and documentation are still being made. The earlier we're involved, the more of those decisions you control.

The insurer offered me a settlement. Should I take it?

Not before you understand what your claim is actually worth. First offers are calculated to close files cheaply. A free consultation gives you a second opinion before you sign anything permanent.

Do you really handle cases in Spanish?

Yes — the entire case, not just the intake call. Consultations, paperwork, hearings preparation, and every conversation in between can be handled in Spanish.
